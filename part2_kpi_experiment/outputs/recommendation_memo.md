# Executive Recommendation Memo

*To:* Executive Leadership Team  
*From:* Lead Business Analyst  
*Date:* June 23, 2026  
*Subject:* Activation & Onboarding Optimization Campaign Deployment Strategy  

## 1. Executive Summary
This memorandum delivers the final data-driven recommendation regarding the full-scale deployment of the new user onboarding and activation campaign. Based on rigorous statistical analysis of the 1,408 experimental profiles, the campaign operates as an exceptionally strong conversion engine—statistically increasing our North Star *Paid Conversion Rate* from *3.19% to 7.04%* ($p = 0.0009$). 

However, severe downstream operational risks have emerged. The campaign triggered a dramatic spike in the *Support Ticket Rate* from *14.78% to 24.79%, alongside introducing customer refunds. Consequently, a universal launch is **rejected* at this time in favor of a *Targeted Segmented Launch & Iterative Testing* strategy.

## 2. Core Experiment Performance Framework
The structural outcomes across our KPI tree layers demonstrate clear behavioral trade-offs between initial user activation and downstream support sustainability:

- *North Star Metric (Paid Conversion Rate):* Achieved an absolute lift of *+3.85%* (more than doubling the control group's baseline performance).
- *Funnel Velocity:* Accelerated user time-to-conversion, reducing the average days to convert from *8.86 Days to 6.40 Days*.
- *Product Activation:* Driven higher baseline engagement, lifting the *Average Engagement Score* from *57.02 to 62.93*.

## 3. Hypothesis Testing Summary
- *Null Hypothesis ($H_0$):* Rejected ($P_{\text{Treatment}} \neq P_{\text{Control}}$)
- *Statistical Evidence:* Two-Proportion Z-Test yielded a computed *Z-Statistic of 3.3165* and a *P-Value of 0.0009*.
- *Conclusion:* The positive shift in the conversion rate is mathematically significant and cleared our pre-set alpha threshold ($\alpha = 0.05$). The conversion lift is a direct result of the new onboarding experience.

## 4. Operational Guardrail Assessment & Risks
Universal deployment is currently blocked by critical operational health indicators:
- *Support Ticket Escalation:* The *+10.01% absolute surge* in customer support tickets confirms that the new journey introduces extensive post-signup friction, technical issues, or missing information.
- *Revenue Quality Risk:* The emergence of a *0.42% refund rate* in the Treatment group warns that the activation experience may be overselling product capabilities, causing rapid buyer’s remorse once the user crosses the paywall.

## 5. Segment-Level Deep Dive
Granular multi-dimensional analysis indicates that performance was not uniform across user devices:
- *Mobile Cohort Dominance:* Mobile users experienced the most dramatic conversion acceleration, jumping from *2.57% to 7.34%* (a 185% relative increase).
- *Desktop/Tablet Stagnation:* While positive, alternative hardware streams demonstrated tighter conversion margins, suggesting the visual layout is highly optimized for smaller mobile aspect ratios but lacks matching efficacy on screens with larger real estate.

## 6. Final Strategic Decision & Next Steps
*Strategic Recommendation: LAUNCH ONLY FOR SELECTED SEGMENT (MOBILE) & CONTINUE EXPERIMENTAL ITERATION.*

The campaign should not be rolled out universally across all platforms due to the operational strain it puts on support teams. Instead, we recommend a phased approach:

### Immediate Action Plan:
1. *Execute Phased Mobile Rollout:* Capitalize on the exceptional mobile performance by scaling the treatment experience exclusively for Mobile app interfaces.
2. *Onboarding UX Audit:* Halt the Desktop/Tablet rollout and immediately audit the onboarding steps to eliminate the friction points causing the 10% support ticket surge.
3. *Align Marketing Copy:* Adjust the initial onboarding micro-copy to better align expectations with reality, neutralizing the refund triggers before conducting the next experimental loop.
